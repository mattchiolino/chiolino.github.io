Mike's Popcorn

Mike's is a family-owned popcorn company located in Kaukauna, Wisconsin who has been serving up classic, mouthwatering popcorn flavors since 1968.

Tags

Design

Team

Randall Reiter, Sasa Palavra

Date

May 2013 — Sep 2013

Background

While working as a contract graphic designer for a local ad agency, I was tasked with the creation of a new, updated logo and product labels for a popcorn company in Kaukauna, Wisconsin.

Initial Research

The logo had to be fresh and clean, while still giving a nod to the old logo, which the owner (Mike) had a soft-spot for. Additionally, the logo was to be used in all Mike’s Popcorn branding — including a new website, Facebook page, packaging, and all other print applications.

First off, I took the time to assess which pieces of the old logo were working, and which were not. I could tell that this logo design was not a professional job, but there were plenty of elements that were interesting enough for me to consider retaining as I explored different options. The color was definitely a good starting point, as I did not want to mess with any core brand-recognizable elements. Additionally, while the font was definitely over-designed, with its gradients, beveling, and drop shadows, the general style of “chunky playful serif” was definitely a tribute to the owner’s personality — fun-loving, spontaneous, yet grounded in tradition. I knew I’d want to retain this feel with the new design.

Ideation

Sketches

When I started sketching concepts, I considered multiple layout options while adhering to the constraints above. Eventually, I settled on 3 solid options I knew I wanted to put in front of the client.

Design

The clover is a hand-drawn element that can be used in a variety of applications; namely, if you visit the Mike’s Popcorn site, you’ll notice its use in your browser tab’s icon at the top. I chose a lighter green for the fill, and the primary dark green for a stroke. The cream color for the background of the mark is intended to reflect the caremel-ly deliciousness of their sweet corns, as well as the cheesy, salty, finger-licking awesomeness of their more salty flavors.

In addition to the logo, I was tasked with creating product labels for their flavors. All follow a consistent, recognizable pattern, with the exception of a small change to the Party Mix bag. Tilting, overlapping, and offsetting the characters for this mix gives the impression that this combination is a little quirky, but amazing all the same.
